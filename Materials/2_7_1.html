Basic Statistics and Random Variables

Measures of Central Tendency

Central tendency refers to the middle point or typical value of a dataset.

  • Mean (Average): Sum of all values divided by the number of values.
  • Median: Middle value when the data is arranged in order.
  • Mode: Most frequently occurring value in a dataset.
Example:
Dataset: 2, 4, 4, 4, 5, 6, 8
Mean = (2+4+4+4+5+6+8)/7 = 4.71
Median = 4
Mode = 4

Moments

Moments are quantitative measures related to the shape of the data distribution.

  • First Moment: Mean
  • Second Moment: Variance
  • Third Moment: Skewness (asymmetry)
  • Fourth Moment: Kurtosis (peakedness)

Skewness and Kurtosis

Skewness

Skewness measures the asymmetry of the data distribution.

  • Positive Skew: Tail on the right side is longer.
  • Negative Skew: Tail on the left side is longer.

Kurtosis

Kurtosis measures the "tailedness" or the sharpness of the peak of the data distribution.

  • Leptokurtic: Sharper peak than normal distribution.
  • Platykurtic: Flatter peak than normal distribution.
  • Mesokurtic: Normal distribution kurtosis.

Random Variables

A random variable assigns a real number to each outcome of a random experiment.

Types

  • Discrete Random Variables: Take countable values (e.g., number of heads in coin toss).
  • Continuous Random Variables: Take an infinite number of possible values (e.g., height, weight).

Probability Functions

  • Probability Mass Function (PMF): For discrete random variables, gives the probability of each value.
  • Probability Density Function (PDF): For continuous random variables, describes the likelihood of a value within a given range.
Example (PMF):
Tossing a fair coin: P(X=0) = 0.5, P(X=1) = 0.5

Constants of Random Variables

Mean (Expected Value)

The mean is the long-run average value of repetitions of the experiment.

Variance

Variance measures the spread of the data around the mean.

Moments about Mean

The nth central moment is the expected value of (X - mean)^n.

Bivariate Distributions and Covariance

Bivariate Distribution

It deals with the probability distribution involving two random variables simultaneously.

Covariance

Covariance measures the directional relationship between two random variables.

  • Positive Covariance: Variables move in the same direction.
  • Negative Covariance: Variables move in opposite directions.
Example:
If high marks in Math tend to go with high marks in Physics, covariance is positive.
