Semiconductors and Optoelectronic Devices

Radiative & Non-radiative Transitions

Radiative Transitions

Transitions where an electron changes energy levels and emits a photon. Examples:

  • Spontaneous Emission: Electron spontaneously returns to a lower energy state, emitting a photon.
  • Stimulated Emission: Incoming photon triggers electron transition, emitting a coherent photon. Principle behind lasers.
  • Recombination: Electron recombines with a hole, emitting a photon.

Non-radiative Transitions

Transitions where an electron changes energy levels without emitting a photon; energy is dissipated as heat (phonons). Examples:

  • Shockley-Read-Hall (SRH) Recombination: Through defect levels.
  • Auger Recombination: Energy transferred to another electron, lost as heat.

LED, PIN Photodetector, Avalanche Photodetector & Solar Cell

LED (Light Emitting Diode)

A semiconductor device that emits light when current passes through it, produced by radiative recombination. Light color depends on bandgap energy.

PIN Photodetector

A photodetector with a wide intrinsic semiconductor region between p-type and n-type regions, enhancing photon absorption and responsivity. Absorbed photons generate electron-hole pairs that create current under an electric field.

Avalanche Photodetector (APD)

A photodetector using impact ionization to amplify photocurrent. High reverse bias enables carriers to generate additional electron-hole pairs, creating an avalanche effect and large gain.

Solar Cell

A semiconductor device that converts sunlight into electricity via the photovoltaic effect. Photon absorption generates electron-hole pairs, separated by the built-in electric field, creating voltage and current.
