Homogeneous and Non-homogeneous Linear Differential Equations

Homogeneous Equations

A homogeneous linear differential equation with constant coefficients has the form:

a_ndny/dxn + a_{n-1}dn-1y/dxn-1 + ⋯ + a_1dydx + a_0y = 0

Solution Method:

  • Assume a solution of the form \( y = e^{rx} \).
  • Substituting into the equation gives the characteristic equation:

    \( a_n r^n + a_{n-1} r^{n-1} + \cdots + a_1 r + a_0 = 0 \)

  • The nature of the roots determines the general solution:
    • Distinct Real Roots \( r_1, r_2, \dots, r_n \):

      \( y(x) = c_1 e^{r_1 x} + c_2 e^{r_2 x} + \cdots + c_n e^{r_n x} \)

    • Repeated Roots \( r \) repeated \( k \) times:

      \( y(x) = (c_1 + c_2 x + \cdots + c_k x^{k-1})e^{rx} \)

    • Complex Conjugate Roots \( \alpha \pm i\beta \):

      \( y(x) = e^{\alpha x} (c_1 \cos(\beta x) + c_2 \sin(\beta x)) \)

Example:

Solve \( y'' - 3y' + 2y = 0 \).

Characteristic equation: \( r^2 - 3r + 2 = 0 \) which factors to \( (r-1)(r-2) = 0 \).

Roots: \( r_1 = 1 \), \( r_2 = 2 \).

General solution: \( y(x) = c_1 e^{x} + c_2 e^{2x} \).

Non-homogeneous Equations

A non-homogeneous linear differential equation has the form:

\( a_n \frac{d^n y}{dx^n} + a_{n-1} \frac{d^{n-1} y}{dx^{n-1}} + \cdots + a_1 \frac{dy}{dx} + a_0 y = f(x) \)

The general solution is:
\( y(x) = y_c(x) + y_p(x) \)

Method of Undetermined Coefficients:

  1. Guess the form of \( y_p(x) \) based on \( f(x) \).
  2. Substitute into the differential equation and solve for unknown coefficients.

Example:

Solve \( y'' - 3y' + 2y = 3e^{2x} \).

Complementary solution: \( y_c(x) = c_1 e^{x} + c_2 e^{2x} \).

Particular solution guess: \( y_p(x) = Axe^{2x} \).

After solving: \( A = 3 \), thus \( y_p(x) = 3x e^{2x} \).

General solution: \( y(x) = c_1 e^{x} + c_2 e^{2x} + 3x e^{2x} \).

Variation of Parameters

Useful when undetermined coefficients do not work. For an equation:

\( y'' + p(x) y' + q(x) y = f(x) \)

Steps:

  1. Find \( y_c(x) = c_1 y_1(x) + c_2 y_2(x) \).
  2. Compute the Wronskian:
    \( W(y_1, y_2) = y_1 y_2' - y_2 y_1' \)
  3. Find \( u_1(x) \) and \( u_2(x) \):
    \( u_1(x) = -\int \frac{y_2(x) f(x)}{W(y_1, y_2)} dx \)
    \( u_2(x) = \int \frac{y_1(x) f(x)}{W(y_1, y_2)} dx \)
  4. Particular solution: \( y_p(x) = u_1(x) y_1(x) + u_2(x) y_2(x) \).

Example:

Solve \( y'' + y = \sec(x) \).

Complementary solution: \( y_c(x) = c_1 \cos(x) + c_2 \sin(x) \).

Wronskian: \( W = 1 \).

Find \( u_1(x) \) and \( u_2(x) \):
\( u_1(x) = \ln|\cos(x)| \),
\( u_2(x) = x \).

Particular solution: \( y_p(x) = \cos(x) \ln|\cos(x)| + x \sin(x) \).

General solution: \( y(x) = c_1 \cos(x) + c_2 \sin(x) + \cos(x) \ln|\cos(x)| + x \sin(x) \).

Equations with Variable Coefficients

Cauchy-Euler Equations:

Form:
\( ax^2 y'' + bxy' + cy = 0 \)

Assume \( y = x^r \), leading to the auxiliary equation:
\( ar(r-1) + br + c = 0 \)

Solutions:

  • Distinct real roots \( r_1, r_2 \): \( y(x) = c_1 x^{r_1} + c_2 x^{r_2} \)
  • Repeated root \( r_1 \): \( y(x) = (c_1 + c_2 \ln|x|) x^{r_1} \)
  • Complex conjugate roots \( \alpha \pm i\beta \): \( y(x) = x^\alpha (c_1 \cos(\beta \ln|x|) + c_2 \sin(\beta \ln|x|)) \)

Example:

Solve \( x^2 y'' + 4x y' + 2y = 0 \).

Auxiliary equation: \( r(r-1) + 4r + 2 = 0 \) â‡’ \( r^2 + 3r + 2 = 0 \).
Roots: \( r_1 = -1 \), \( r_2 = -2 \).

General solution: \( y(x) = c_1 x^{-1} + c_2 x^{-2} \).

Legendre's Equation:

Form:
\( (1-x^2)y'' - 2x y' + n(n+1)y = 0 \)

Solutions are Legendre polynomials \( P_n(x) \).

First few Legendre polynomials:

  • \( P_0(x) = 1 \)
  • \( P_1(x) = x \)
  • \( P_2(x) = \frac{1}{2}(3x^2 - 1) \)
  • \( P_3(x) = \frac{1}{2}(5x^3 - 3x) \)

General solution:
\( y(x) = c_1 P_n(x) + c_2 Q_n(x) \)
where \( Q_n(x) \) is the Legendre function of the second kind.
