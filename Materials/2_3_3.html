Elementary Combinatorics

Basics of Counting

  • Rule of Sum: If there are m ways to do something and n ways to do another, and these two cannot happen together, then there are m + n ways to choose one of them.
  • Rule of Product: If there are m ways to do something and n ways to do another, and both can happen together, then there are m × n ways to do both.

Combinations and Permutations

Permutations

Arrangement of objects in a specific order.

Formula:
P(n, r) = n! / (n - r)!
Where:
- n = total objects
- r = objects taken at a time

Combinations

Selection of objects without regard to the order.

Formula:
C(n, r) = n! / (r! × (n - r)!)

Permutations and Combinations with Repetitions

Permutations with Repetitions

When repetition is allowed:

Formula:
n^r
Where:
- n = types of items
- r = number of selections

Combinations with Repetitions

Choosing items where repetition is allowed:

Formula:
C(n + r - 1, r)

Constrained Repetitions

In constrained repetition problems, certain restrictions are placed on the number of times an item can be repeated.

Example: Forming numbers from digits where certain digits must appear only once.

Binomial Coefficients

Binomial coefficients appear in the expansion of powers of a binomial expression.

Formula:
C(n, r) = n! / (r! × (n - r)!)

Properties:

  • Symmetry: C(n, r) = C(n, n - r)
  • Sum of coefficients: 2n (Sum of all C(n, r))

Binomial and Multinomial Theorems

Binomial Theorem

Describes the algebraic expansion of powers of a binomial.

(a + b)^n = Σ [C(n, k) × a^(n-k) × b^k]
for k = 0 to n

Multinomial Theorem

Generalization of the binomial theorem to multiple variables.

(x1 + x2 + ... + xm)^n = Σ [n! / (k1! k2! ... km!) × x1^k1 × x2^k2 × ... × xm^km]
Where:
- Sum of ki = n

Principle of Inclusion–Exclusion

Used to calculate the size of the union of several sets.

|A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| - |A ∩ B|
|A ∪ B ∪ C| = |A| + |B| + |C| - |A ∩ B| - |A ∩ C| - |B ∩ C| + |A ∩ B ∩ C|

Application:

  • Counting the number of elements in a union of sets.
  • Solving complex counting problems with overlapping categories.
