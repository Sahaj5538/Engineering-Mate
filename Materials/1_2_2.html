Semiconductors and Carrier Transport

Intrinsic & Extrinsic Semiconductors

Intrinsic Semiconductors

Pure semiconductors like silicon (Si) or germanium (Ge), with no impurities. In an intrinsic semiconductor, the number of electrons (n) equals the number of holes (p), and both are equal to the intrinsic carrier concentration (ni).

Extrinsic Semiconductors

Semiconductors doped with impurities to alter electrical properties:

  • n-type: Doped with donor impurities (e.g., phosphorus in silicon), making electrons the majority carriers (n ≫ p).
  • p-type: Doped with acceptor impurities (e.g., boron in silicon), making holes the majority carriers (p ≫ n).

Carrier Transport: Diffusion and Drift

Diffusion

Movement of charge carriers from a region of high concentration to low concentration.

Diffusion current densities:

  • Jn = qDn (dn/dx)
  • Jp = -qDp (dp/dx)

Where:

  • q ≈ 1.602 × 10-19 C (elementary charge)
  • Dn and Dp are diffusion coefficients for electrons and holes

Drift

Movement of charge carriers due to an electric field (E).

Drift current densities:

  • Jn = qnμnE
  • Jp = qpμpE

Where:

  • μn and μp are mobilities of electrons and holes, respectively.
  • n and p are carrier concentrations.

p-n Junction Diode I-V Characteristics

A p-n junction diode is formed by joining p-type and n-type semiconductors. Its I-V characteristics are described by the Shockley diode equation:

I = Is (eqV/nkT - 1)

Where:

  • I is the diode current
  • Is is the reverse saturation current
  • V is the applied voltage
  • q is the elementary charge
  • n is the ideality factor (typically between 1 and 2)
  • k is Boltzmann's constant
  • T is the absolute temperature

Zener Diode I-V Characteristics

A Zener diode is designed to operate in the reverse breakdown region. Its characteristics:

  • Forward Bias: Behaves like a normal diode.
  • Reverse Bias: Blocks current until breakdown voltage (VZ) is reached, then conducts while maintaining a nearly constant voltage.

Hall Effect & Applications

Hall Effect: Production of a Hall voltage (VH) across a conductor when an electric current flows through it in the presence of a perpendicular magnetic field.

Hall voltage equation:

VH = (BI) / (nqt)

Where:

  • B is the magnetic field strength
  • I is the current
  • n is the charge carrier density
  • q is the elementary charge
  • t is the thickness of the conductor

Applications: Determine type, density, and mobility of charge carriers in semiconductors.
