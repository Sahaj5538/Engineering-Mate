Atomic and Molecular Orbitals

Definition

Atomic Orbitals

Mathematical functions describing the probability of finding an electron around an atom's nucleus, characterized by quantum numbers (principal, azimuthal, magnetic, and spin). Specific shapes and energy levels:

  • s orbitals: Spherical (e.g., 1s, 2s)
  • p orbitals: Dumbbell-shaped (e.g., 2px, 2py, 2pz)
  • d orbitals: Complex shapes (e.g., dxy, dyz, dxz, dx²-y², dz²)
  • f orbitals: Even more complex shapes

Molecular Orbitals

Formed by the combination of atomic orbitals when atoms form molecules:

  • Sigma (σ) orbitals: Head-on overlap
  • Pi (π) orbitals: Lateral overlap
  • Bonding orbitals: Lower energy, increase stability
  • Antibonding orbitals: Higher energy, decrease stability (denoted by an asterisk, e.g., σ*, π*)

Comparison

Molecular Orbital Theory

Postulates

  • Atomic orbitals combine to form molecular orbitals.
  • Number of MOs = number of AOs combined.
  • Bonding MOs are lower in energy; antibonding MOs are higher.
  • Electrons fill MOs following Aufbau principle, Hund's rule, and Pauli exclusion principle.
  • Molecular stability depends on bonding vs. antibonding electrons.

Theories of Metallic Bonding

Free Electron Theory

Valence electrons are delocalized across the metal lattice. Electrons are free, leading to metallic conductivity.

Limitations: Cannot explain cohesive forces or differences in properties.

Resonance Theory

Metallic bonding is viewed as resonance hybrids of multiple covalent bond structures.

Molecular Orbital Theory

Extends MO theory to metals, forming continuous bands of energy levels.

Valence Bond Theory

Electrons are shared between atoms, similar to covalent bonds, but less successful at explaining metallic properties.

Bonding in Coordination Complexes

  • [Ni(CO)₄]: Tetrahedral, Ni(0), strong σ donation and π back-bonding.
  • [NiCl₄]²⁻: Tetrahedral, Ni(+2), high-spin with weak-field ligands.
  • [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻: Square planar, Ni(+2), strong-field ligands, low-spin.
  • [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺: Octahedral, Co(+3), strong-field, low-spin complex.
  • [CoF₆]³⁻: Octahedral, Co(+3), weak-field, high-spin complex.

Crystal Field Theory

Crystal Field Splitting

Splitting of d-orbitals due to ligand interactions; energy difference is called crystal field splitting energy (Δ).

Octahedral Geometry

d-orbitals split into:

  • eg: higher energy (dx²–y², dz²)
  • t2g: lower energy (dxy, dyz, dxz)

Crystal field splitting energy: Δo

Tetrahedral Geometry

d-orbitals split into:

  • e: lower energy (dx²–y², dz²)
  • t2: higher energy (dxy, dyz, dxz)

Crystal field splitting: Δt ≈ 4/9 Δo

Square Planar Geometry

d-orbitals split into four levels:

  • dx²–y² (highest energy)
  • dxy
  • dz²
  • dxz, dyz (lowest energy)
